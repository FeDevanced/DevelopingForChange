Jack Doohan

Reserve Driver. #7

Not every important decision in a racing career happens when the lights go out. Sometimes the most significant move is the one you make when you're not in the car.

Jack Doohan is twenty-three years old, has seven Formula 1 race starts to his name, and has joined TGR Haas as Official Reserve Driver for 2026. For anyone paying close attention, this isn't a consolation role. It's a deliberate, clear-eyed decision from a driver who knows exactly where he wants to be, and has chosen the most direct route to getting back there.

Where He Comes From

Doohan was born in Queensland, Australia, into a family that already knew a thing or two about winning at the highest level of motorsport. His father, Mick Doohan, is a five-time 500cc MotoGP World Champion. Remarkably, Jack's first kart was a gift from Michael Schumacher. It would be easy to frame his career as one defined by that background. It isn't. He earned his own way through the junior categories on his own terms.

He finished runner-up in the FIA Formula 3 championship in 2021, raced in Formula 2 for two seasons with Virtuosi, taking multiple victories and finishing third in the 2023 standings, and spent two years as Alpine's reserve driver accumulating free practice sessions, private tests, and a thorough education in what it takes to operate inside an F1 team.

His Time in Formula 1

Doohan made his race debut at the 2024 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix and started the 2025 season as Alpine's race driver, completing six grands prix from Australia to Miami. He showed flashes of the ability that made him one of the most compelling junior talents of his generation. When Alpine decided to make a change, Doohan returned to a reserve role and spent the rest of the season on the sidelines, watching, learning, and waiting.

At the end of 2025, he and Alpine parted ways. He arrived at TGR Haas shortly after.

Why Move to Haas?

This is a team with a growing test programme, a technical partnership that is bringing more cars and more running than Haas has ever had access to before, and a team principal who has stated clearly that Doohan's contribution is valued. For a driver who hasn't competed in a full season since 2023, staying sharp and staying visible matters. TGR Haas gives him both.

There are seats on the 2027 grid to be filled. Doohan intends to be in one of them. In the meantime, he is here, working, and ready.
