Our 2026 Challenger: VF-26

Every generation of Formula 1 eventually hits a reset. New regulations, new architecture, a new set of problems for every team on the grid to solve simultaneously. In 2026, that moment arrived, and we were ready for it.

The VF-26 is our answer. Built from scratch under the 2026 technical regulations, it's a car that's smaller, lighter, and more agile than anything we've put on the grid before. It's also the first TGR Haas machine, carrying the identity of a team that's not just changed its name but changed what it's capable of building.

A New Kind of Formula 1 Car

The 2026 regulations didn't tweak the rulebook. They rewrote it. Cars are narrower, sitting at 1,900mm overall width, down from the previous 2,000mm. They're lighter, tipping the scales at 770kg with the driver. The aerodynamic philosophy has shifted too, with reduced downforce and drag placing a greater premium on mechanical precision and driver skill. The front and rear wings look markedly different from the previous generation, and new bodywork regulations mean every car on the grid had to be conceived from a blank page.

We started that process in the second half of 2024. While the rest of the team was still fighting in the midfield with the VF-25, a small group was already working on the concept of what would become this car. Resources shifted gradually toward the 2026 programme across last season, fully switching over after the summer break. Getting two live car programmes across the line at once is one of the harder things a technical operation can be asked to do. We did it.

The Power Unit

At the heart of the VF-26 sits the Ferrari O67/6, a turbocharged 1.6 litre V6 hybrid running on 100% sustainable fuel. What makes the 2026 power unit genuinely new is the energy split: for the first time in Formula 1, the balance between internal combustion and electrical power is near-even. Managing that split across a race distance, knowing when to deploy, when to conserve, and how to protect performance on the straights under the new Overtake and Boost systems, is one of the defining challenges of this season. Every team is learning it simultaneously. The ones who master it fastest will have a real advantage.

Built to Fight

The VF-26 isn't here to make up the numbers. The team that built it worked long days and late nights, not because they were told to, but because they wanted to. That's the culture inside this garage, and it shows up in the car.

2026 is a clean slate for every team on the grid. We intend to make the most of it.
