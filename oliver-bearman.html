Oliver Bearman

Driver. Car #87

There are drivers who earn their way onto the Formula 1 grid quietly, through years of results and patience, and then there are drivers who announce themselves in a moment nobody saw coming. Ollie Bearman is the second kind.

Born in Chelmsford, Essex, in 2005, Bearman grew up in a family with motorsport in its DNA. He started karting at eight years old and was immediately hard to ignore. By sixteen, he had left school and moved to Modena, Italy, to join the Ferrari Driver Academy, choosing racing over everything else at an age when most people are still figuring out what they want to do.

The Junior Years

He didn't waste any time. In 2021 he became the first driver in history to win both the German and Italian Formula 4 championships in the same season. From there, a strong Formula 3 rookie campaign with Prema led directly into Formula 2, where he was competitive from the outset, winning races across two seasons while continuing to develop inside one of the most demanding academies in the sport.

He was building a case for Formula 1 the patient way. Then, in March 2024, the phone rang.

The Debut That Changed Everything

With Carlos Sainz hospitalised ahead of the Saudi Arabian Grand Prix, Ferrari needed a driver at less than a day's notice. They called Bearman. He was eighteen years old, had never sat in an F1 car on a race weekend, and was in the middle of an F2 campaign. He qualified eleventh, missing Q3 by 0.036 seconds from Lewis Hamilton, and finished seventh in the race, outrunning both Hamilton and Lando Norris before the chequered flag.

It was one of the more remarkable debuts in the history of the sport. More importantly, it was a statement of intent from a driver who hadn't flinched for a single lap.

He signed for Haas on a multi-year deal before the year was out.

What He Brings to TGR Haas F1 Team

Bearman's first full season in 2025 followed the arc you'd expect from a driver of his calibre. He learned, he adapted, and in the second half of the year he started delivering, taking fourth place at the Mexico City Grand Prix to match the best result in the team's history. He finished the season thirteenth in the championship with 41 points, bringing the kind of raw pace and intelligent racecraft that a team building toward something genuinely needs.

Now in his second year, the foundations are laid. The potential was never in doubt. The question for 2026 is how far he can go with a car built around the biggest regulation reset in years, and a team around him that is growing at the same rate he is.
